PPM and PCM signal coding in Futaba systems

 

After writing this page, I was informed about one page (much better than mine) in Internet about different R/C modulations:
http://www.aerodesign.de/peter/2000/PCM/PCM_PPM_eng.html

Everything in this page is just measured from the existing system and the written text is just based on assumptions. There is no confirmation from FUTABA for this information. All additions and bugs are welcome (risto.koiva@web.de).

The info is measured at the output of ROBBE/FUTABA FC-28 v.3.2 Trainer4 module with TEKTRONIX THS720 digital oscilloscope.

PCM Signal (Pulse-Code-Modulation)

Notice the 28ms long frame. The frame starts or ends with 3ms high-pulse (reset) - it depends how to look. In the following I have taken that the frame starts with the reset pulse. The principle is similar to PPM.

Close-up of the start of the frame:

New bit is sent every 300µs. One can read from the above picture: reset-00101010010111...
The first bits in the frame are in my opinion the frame indicator. For normal frames they seem to be: 00101
The other frame types are for failsafe data and channels above 8 (ch9 and ch10 in FC-28 etc.)

In normal frame, after the frame indicator, comes the channel position data. All 1-8 channels are sent in sequence, each with 10-bit (1024 position resolution). At the end of the frame is one low bit (0). I am also assuming that the value 1111111111 is not allowed (would equal to reset).

I have not found any parity or ECC info in the normal frame (but have not looked into it for very long also).
